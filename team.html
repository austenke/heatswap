Heatswap was dreamed up…

by a small team of dedicated Sneakerheads who live, buy and sell shoes in San Francisco. Unhappy with the hassle of CraigsList, the high fees of Amazon, and the coldness of eBay, we decided to start a safe, friendly, and easy to use site for people who can appreciate how fine it feels to get just the right pair of sneakers.

We also wanted to see if we could team up with other Sneakerheads and generate some good. The team has pledged to give away a percentage of every sale to our non-profit partners, all of whom work on behalf of kids who need a break.

Austen Keene
technical director

Austen first ran into programming when he found an old HTML book in 6th grade. He proceeded to teach himself JavaScript and CSS, working in tech shops and on personal projects. He's a top contributor for Appium, an open source mobile testing application, and primarily does backend development. When he's not programming he is fencing, playing video games, or binge watching tv.

Clare Ellis
general manager

Clare used to think sneakers were for running. Having wised up she is happy to be putting her product and media experience to work, giving sneaker entrepreneurs a new platform for buying and selling shoes too good to run in. She has worked for MTV, Time-Warner, and Disney Publishing and sold one of her start-ups to Nickelodeon. When she’s not thinking about how HeatSwap can survive beyond its own start-up phase she likes to help out at a local shelter and work with kids at College Track. Favorite pair of shoes: Air Jordan “BRED” 4

Gordy Webb
promotions and partnerships director

Gordy learned to appreciate sneakers when he was in elementary school and had to wear a uniform. What he wore on his feet became a creative outlet. He has traded in sneakers since sixth grade and started Calikix, a shoe customizing business, after 8th grade, with clients that include Royal Respect. When he’s not getting out the good word about HeatSwap, he produces music for Face Jam with fellow HeatSwapper Jon Anderson. What he looks for in a sneaker: authenticity.

Jayraj Govender
media manager

Jayraj started making films right around the time he started buying and selling sneakers and loves the chance to bring together his two passions for HeatSwap. He has made films and music videos for clients that include Lick-Wilmerding High School and Face Jam. When he isn’t working on finding the perfect backdrop for a pair of Supreme Foamposites, he’s skateboarding.

Jody Duncan
tech associate

Jody does freelance web design and front-end development from his apartment in San Francisco or at a coffee shop down the street. He had never heard the term Sneakerheads before meeting the HeatSwap crew but is now a believer. Favorite shoe: Cncpts x Asics Gel Lyte V

Jon Anderson
media associate

Jon started his love affair with photography as a fourth grader when he borrowed his dad's camera to take pictures of his friends. Since then he has worked on the sets of Dawn of the Planet of the Apes, Pacific Rim, and The Internship. When he's not taking photos with he's most likely enjoying three Cool Ranch® Doritos® Locos Tacos at his local Taco Bell. Favorite shoe: Statue of Liberty Dunks

Maddy Leibovitch
design associate

Maddy L. was 12 when she created her first website but her lifelong passion is art and design. HeatSwap is the perfect opportunity to merge her two interests in the creation of a rad site that combines both function and form. When she’s not working on the site, you can find Maddy thrift-shopping and reviewing best spots to eat great food in the Bay Area. Favorite pair of shoes: Kobe 5 Think Pink

Maddy Nakada
tech associate

Maddy N. designed her first website, an online store for her school, in 6th grade. Since then she has strayed from web design to focus on robotics. Heatswap has given her the chance to return to the world of web design. While not programming, Maddy enjoys running competitively and making Banh Mi sandwiches. Favorite pair of shoes: Nike Roshe Run
