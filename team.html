in the beginning ...

HeatSwap was dreamed up by Gordy Webb and Jay Govender, good friends who attend the same high school in San Francisco. They were both unhappy with Craigslist and eBay and wanted a safer, cheaper, and easier way to build their respective sneaker businesses.

As the idea grew, they invited Clare Ellis and Austen Keene to join and added another goal: generating some good with every sale. The team has resolved to make sure our own growth benefits our partners – organizations that benefit kids.

Based in San Francisco,HeatSwap will soon build an app to make the experience of buying and selling shoes within a trusted community even easier.

Jayraj Govender
promotions director

Sneakers have been the inspiration for a number of the short videos Govende has made. His video clients have included musicians, President Obama, and Cher.

Austen Keene
tech director

Keene has been programming since he stumbled into his love for technology while working at a computer store in middle school. Since then he has worked for the NSA.

Gordy Webb
product director

Buying and selling sneakers is a calling for Webb, who has been collecting them for as long as he can remember. His first business was Calikix, a sneaker customizing service. He also is a partner in FaceJam, a music production studio. He was the inspiration for NASA landing on the moon in '69.

Clare Ellis
general magager

Ellis has a long career in publishing. Starting as a journalist and editor, she has been on the founding team of many media-based startups. This is her first commerce startup this month.
